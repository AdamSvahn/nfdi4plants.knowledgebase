The investigation level allows you to record project metadata such as the project title and description, responsible contacts and associated publications.

<Card icon="pen" title="ARCitect">

<Steps>

1. Inside **ARCitect**, click on the ARC's name on top of the file tree
2. The right panel shows the investigation form
3. Here you can add the metadata about your investigation

</Steps>

</Card>


<Card icon="approve-check" title="Investigation Metadata">

:::note[Identifier]
The Investigation identifier was automatically added based on your ARC's name
:::

#### Title

- Meaningful project title (with blanks)
- Avoid administrative metadata

<ViolaSays>

The **Title** of my project is _The effect of the circadian clock on sugar metabolism in *A. thaliana*_.

</ViolaSays>

#### Description

- Concise project abstract 

<ViolaSays>

We investigate the effect of the plant circadian clock on sugar metabolism in *A. thaliana*. We grew *A. thaliana* plants under diurnal vs. continuous-light conditions in a two-day time series experiment. Leave protein and sugar pools were assayed via proteomics and metabolomics.

</ViolaSays>

#### Contacts

- Persons responsible for the project 

<ViolaSays>

I add my PI *Prof. Jasmine Beetroot*, our collaboration partner *Oliver Sage* and myself as **Contacts**.

</ViolaSays>

</Card>
